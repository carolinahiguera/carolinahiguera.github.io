About Me

I am Full-Time Lecturer at Universidad de los Andes. I am really interested in working on imitation learning and multi-agent RL, motivated by the reward shaping problem. I consider that a key factor for solving it is extracting information about the task directly from demonstrations. Moreover, these demos should not only come from an expert agent, but also from a set of agents already trained on simple and related tasks. In this way, the learner agent can transfer and get a baseline to keep building knowledge in a complex task. Furthermore, I think that this approach can give an insight into how to solve the reward shaping problem in multi-agent scenarios, especially, in those where the interaction between agents is strong, and thereby, the global reward function cannot be decompose straightforward into contributions per agent. Currently, I am looking for a Ph.D. that can help me study further around these ideas.
